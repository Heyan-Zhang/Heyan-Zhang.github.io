<span class='anchor' id='about-me'></span>

I am a second-year Ph.D. student in the [Department of Statistics](https://stat.wisc.edu/) at the University of Wisconsin-Madison, fortunately advised by [Prof. Yongyi Guo](https://sites.google.com/view/yongyi-guo/) . I obtained my M.S. degree in Statistics and Data Science from UW-Madison. Prior to that, I obtained my B.S. degree in Statistics from Nankai University.


I am actively engaged in research at the intersection of sequential decision learning and statistics. My primary interests include reinforcement learning, contextual bandits, and model calibration. I am also exploring the practical applications of these methods, especially the use of reinforcement learning in healthcare. Additionally, I have a strong interest in Large Language Models (LLMs) and Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback (RLHF), with experience in areas such as reward modeling and policy optimization.
